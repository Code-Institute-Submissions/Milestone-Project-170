Discover Limerick

Sights

King John's Castle

King John's Castle is a 13th-century castle located on King's Island in Limerick, next to the River Shannon. It is located at the heart of Limerick’s medieval quarter brining to life over 800 years of local history.

Saint Mary's Cathedral

Saint Mary’s Cathedral was founded in 1168 A.D. on a hill on King’s Island, which is the oldest part of Limerick. It is the oldest building in Limerick still in continuous daily use.

Treaty Stone

The Treaty Stone is a monument with a stone said to be used in the signing of the 1691 Treaty of Limerick, marking the surrender of the city to William of Orange. It has rested on a plinth since 1865, at the Clare end of Thomond Bridge.

Riverside Walk

The Limerick City Riverside Walk is a scenic 3.2km shared walking and cycling trail which runs through the historic Shannon Fields and City Centre. There are many historic bridges, including Thomond Bridge and Sarsfield Bridge on this route.

UL Campus

The UL campus is truly one of the most beautiful university campuses anywhere in the world. Visitors never fail to be impressed by this peaceful green place with its trees, its fountains and the beautiful river Shannon flowing gracefully through.

Castletroy Neightbourhood Park

Castletroy Neighbourhood Park features 1.2 km of pedestrian pathways, a fully equipped children’s playground, skateboarding area, circular cycle route and central exhibition area with covering structure.

People's Park

The People's Park is a park located by Pery Square in Limerick. It can be found just west of the railway station and bus terminal. At the northern edge of the park is the Limerick City Gallery of Art.

Sarsfield Bridge

Sarsfield Bridge is a five-span limestone road bridge. It was built between 1824-35 connecting Sarsfield Street and Ennis Road. It was once a swivel bridge that could open allowing large vessels to pass into the upper quays. This function is no longer in use although the mechanisms can still be seen under the bridge.

Activities

Bunratty Castle & Folk Park

Bunratty Castle is a 15th-century castle and living 19th-century village bring the periods alive at this family museum. The castle and the adjoining folk park are run by Shannon Heritage as tourist attractions.

The Hunt Museum

The Hunt Museum is a museum in the city of Limerick. The Hunt Museum holds a personal collection donated by the Hunt family, it was originally situated in the University of Limerick, before being moved to its present location in the Georgian Custom House in 1997.

Limerick City Gallery of Art

Limerick City Gallery of Art is an art museum in the city of Limerick. It is run by Limerick City Council and is located in Pery Square. The gallery is housed in a Romanesque Revival building which was constructed in 1906 as a Carnegie library and museum.

Aura Leisure Grove Island

Aura Leisure Centre is a public leaisure facility with a gym, swimming pool, sauna, steam room and sports hall. Aura has a proven track record in working to support the delivery of public leisure facilities nationwide.

Cratloe Woods

Cratloe Woods is a forested area around the village of Cratloe outside of Limerick City. Much of the original oak forest has been replaced with coniferous softwoods during the past century; however, small pockets of native oak survive. There are several walking and hiking routes to explore.

Limerick Watersports & Activity Centre

The Limerick Watersports & Activity Centre is based in the heart of Limerick City behind the Hunt Museum. A range of water and land based activities are on offer for all ages, abilities and group sizes.

Milk Market

The Milk Market is a covered market selling fruits, vegetables, meats, dairy items, crafts & baked goods since 1852. It provides Limerick residents and visitors with access to a wide variety of fresh, local, in-season products, typically directly from the producer.

Crescent Shopping Centre

The Crescent Shopping Centre is a major shopping centre serving Limerick. It is located in Dooradoyle, on the southern outskirts of the city. There are a number of main anchor tenants, including Tesco, Penneys, Shaws Department Stores, Heatons, GAP Outlet, H&M, Argos and Boots.

Coffee

Hook & Ladder

Hook & Ladder “Living Café” offers customers a unique experience with a variety of store concepts under one roof – café, cookery school, furniture and home accessories store. Hook & Ladder have 4 cafés in Limerick.

Costa Coffee

Costa Coffee is the fastest growing coffee shop chain in the world and loved for its naturally sweet, smooth and distinct flavour. It was founded in London in 1971 by two brothers Bruno and Sergio Costa as a wholesale operation supplying roasted coffee to caterers and specialist Italian coffee shops.

Gloria Jean's Coffees

Gloria Jean's Coffees is an American-Australian retail coffeehouse chain offering gourmet blends in a variety of flavors, plus tea & pastries. It operates more than 1,000 coffee houses in 39 countries

Story Cafe

A coffee shop located in the heart of Limerick on the famous Denmark Street known for great service and atmosphere. An outdoor seating area has been added recently.

Insomnia Coffee Company

Insomnia Coffee Company is an Irish independent coffee chain. The strength lies not only in the quality of the coffee and food served, but also in the experience customers have when they visit.

Starbucks

Starbucks is a coffee chain with more than 15,000 stores in 50 countries. Starbucks is the premier roaster and retailer of specialty coffee in the world. The Starbucks mission to inspire and nurture the human spirit – one person, one cup, and one neighbourhood at a time.

Arabica Coffee Co.

Arabica Coffee Roasters strive to provide world class coffee using the latest in coffee brewing technology and carefully sourced beans. Arabica Coffee Co. offers a consistent and quality product with fast and friendly service.

Café Rosé

Café Rosé is a café & bistro in the heart of Limerick city serving modern cuisine. Café Rosé serves healthy breakfast, lunch, home made desserts to eat in and to go.
