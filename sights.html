Discover Limerick

Sights

King John's Castle

King John's Castle is a 13th-century castle located on King's Island in Limerick, Ireland, next to the River Shannon. Although the site dates back to 922 when the Vikings lived on the Island, the castle itself was built on the orders of King John in 1200.

Milk Market

Airy, covered market selling fruits, veggies, meats, dairy items, crafts & baked goods since 1852.

Treaty Stone

Monument with a stone said to be used in the signing of the 1691 Treaty of Limerick.

Riverside Walk

This scenic 3.2km shared walking and cycling trail which runs through the historic Shannon Fields and City Centre

UL Campus

The UL campus is truly one of the most beautiful university campuses anywhere in the world. Visitors never fail to be impressed by this peaceful green place with its trees, its fountains and the beautiful river Shannon flowing majestically through it all.

Castletroy Neightbourhood Park

Castletroy Neighbourhood Park features 1.2 km of pedestrian pathways, a fully equipped children’s playground, skateboarding area, circular cycle route and central performance/exhibition area with covering structure.

People's Park

The People's Park is a park located by Pery Square in Limerick, Ireland, just west of the railway station and bus terminal. At the northern edge of the park is the Limerick City Gallery of Art.

Sarsfield Bridge

Five-span limestone road bridge, built between 1824-35, spanning the River Shannon joining Sarsfield Street and Ennis Road on a southeast/northwest axis, splaying to both sides to form abutments at the junction with the Ennis Road.

A 15th-century castle and living 19th-century village bring the periods alive at this family museum.

The Hunt Museum

The Hunt Museum is a museum in the city of Limerick, Ireland. The Hunt Museum holds a personal collection donated by the Hunt family, it was originally situated in the University of Limerick, before being moved to its present location in the Georgian Custom House in 1997.

Limerick City Gallery of Art

Limerick City Gallery of Art is an art museum in the city of Limerick, Ireland. It is run by Limerick City Council and is located in Pery Square. The gallery is housed in a Romanesque Revival building which was constructed in 1906 as a Carnegie library and museum.

Aura Leisure Grove Island

Cratloe Woods

Cratloe Woods is a forested area around the village of Cratloe in County Clare, Ireland. Much of the original oak forest has been replaced with coniferous softwoods during the past century; however, small pockets of native oak survive.
